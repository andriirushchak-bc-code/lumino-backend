About the platform

What is Lumino Expense?

Lumino Expense is a mid-market B2B spend-management platform — the kind of product finance teams use to replace paper receipts and email threads. Think of it as the app where you'd normally submit a €42 lunch receipt, track whether it got approved, and eventually get reimbursed on your next paycheck.

The platform at a glance

Lumino handles the full lifecycle of an expense: employees submit, managers approve, Finance reimburses. Every expense has a merchant, category, amount, and status (pending, approved, rejected, or paid). Company policy governs what's reimbursable — a Travel Policy spells out per-diems, hotel limits, and when business-class flights are allowed.

This demo environment has you logged in as Andrii Bondar at Northwind Corp., with a small history of real-looking expenses and your manager Maria Schmidt set up as approver. Everything you see and submit is real-feeling data in a safe sandbox.

So why does a bot matter here?

Because the slowest part of any expense platform isn't the software — it's the friction around it. Finding the right form field. Remembering policy rules. Digging through your inbox for a rejection reason. Copying 40 rows from a spreadsheet into the app one by one.

The Lumino assistant handles all of that through conversation. You ask; it does the work.

What you can ask

Seven things the assistant does well.

Open the chat icon in the bottom-right of the demo page and try any of these. You don't need to phrase things exactly — the bot understands natural language and infers what you mean.

01

List your expenses

See what you've submitted, filtered by how many you want to see at once.

Try asking
"Show me my last 5 expenses."
"Show me my last 3 expenses." "What have I submitted recently?"
02

Check a status

The bot remembers what you just talked about — no need to repeat IDs.

Try asking (after the first one)
"What's the status of the Uber one?"
"Is the Lufthansa flight approved?" "When will Brasserie Lipp be reimbursed?"
03

Submit a new expense

Describe it in one sentence. The bot pulls out the amount, merchant, date, and category.

Try asking
"Add a €42 lunch from yesterday at Brasserie Lipp."
"Submit a €28 taxi ride from last Tuesday." "Log €120 for office supplies today."
04

Find a receipt

Pull up the receipt file for a past expense — or get told gracefully if none is on file.

Try asking
"Where's the receipt for the Berlin hotel?"
"Can I see the Lufthansa receipt?" "Receipt for my WeWork expense?"
05

Ask about policy

Business class on long flights? Per-diem limits? The bot reads the policy docs and answers with the exact section.

Try asking
"Can I expense business class on a 9-hour flight?"
"What's the hotel limit in Europe?" "Are client dinners reimbursable?"
06

Understand a rejection & appeal

When an expense is rejected, ask why — then ask the bot to draft a professional appeal note to your approver.

Try asking
"Why was the Berlin hotel rejected?"
Then: "Yes — say I lost the receipt but I have my booking confirmation."
07

Escalate to Finance

Stuck on something Finance needs to handle? The bot opens a ticket and gives you a reference number.

Try asking
"I still haven't been reimbursed for my February expenses."
"Can someone from Finance review my rejected hotel?"
The signature feature

✨ Smart Import from Google Sheets

The real magic. Paste any Google Sheets link and the bot reads the columns, figures out how they map to Lumino's fields, flags anything ambiguous (like a "F&B" column that could mean meals or entertainment), and bulk-imports once you confirm. No CSV parsing. No copy-paste marathons.

  1. Open the demo and click the chat icon in the bottom-right.
  2. Open this sample Google Sheet (7 rows of fictional Berlin trip expenses) and make your own copy if you want to play with the data. Share settings are already "Anyone with the link — Viewer."
  3. Paste the sheet URL into the chat. The bot will propose a column mapping and flag the F&B category as ambiguous.
  4. Reply with something like "Map F&B to Entertainment, then import." The bot applies your correction and bulk-writes the rows.
Tips that make conversations smoother

Three things worth knowing.

Conversational memory

The bot remembers context

After a list of expenses, you can say "the Uber one" or "the Berlin hotel" — no need to re-paste IDs or full merchant names.

Dates made easy

Use natural language for dates

"Yesterday," "last Tuesday," "April 15" — all understood. No need to type 2026-04-18 unless you want to.

Safety first

Nothing writes without asking

For submissions and imports, the bot always shows what it's about to do and waits for your confirmation before writing to the database.
